Amplifier stuff

I am happy to share details and insight into vintage amplifier restoration. My present focus is documenting amplifiers of Australian origin and sympathetically restoring them - often as a guitar and/or bass amplifier, but sometimes as original. Inherent safety and circuit protection are typically improved or added, and performance is improved where appropriate. This website has sections on
  • Amplifier restorations (including additional material on Williamson amps and Selmer organ)
  • Supporting sound and test equipment
  • Self-split push-pull including Parry Cathamplifier
  • Valve reliability
  • Some other valve related goodies
  • And some articles I've put together
Linked files sometimes get updated, so please refresh your cache (F5) if you want the latest file version.

Audio Engineers 30W PA amplifier circa 1959. Microphone input to 6AU6 pentode stage. 6AU6 pentode stage mixer for Radio and Gram inputs. 12AU7 long-tail PI to KT66 PP common cathode bias, with 18 to 300 ohm speaker tappings. Rack mount chassis.

Australian 5W combo amplifier circa late 1940's. Unknown manufacturer. Microphone input to 6J7 pentode stage and 6V6 SE output stage with Rola output transformer and 8" speaker.

Australian 15W amplifier circa 1952. Unknown manufacturer - probably from Melbourne. Microphone and Pickup inputs via separate front panel 2501 microphone sockets. Switched microphone to 6J5. Summed MIC and PU to 6AU6. 6J7 split-load/concertina PI. 6L6G PP 15W. Ferguson OP58 10k OT. Trimax TP1895 PT with 5V4G rectifier.

Australian 6GW8 amplifier circa late 1960's. Unknown manufacturer - probably a kit of parts. Two 2-pin input sockets. Two 4-pin speaker sockets. Zephyr Mic step-up transformer. EF86 gain stage. 6GW8 triode gain stage and cathodyne PI. 6GW8 pentode, cathode bias PP output stage. Ferguson PF170 PT, and A&R OT2632 OT.

7W Stereo Amplifier circa 1959-1960 - Made by Australian Sound & T.V. for Royal Melbourne Technical College. 6BM8 PP UL 9k output stage; cathodyne PI with direct coupled triode preamp; 12A?7 input preamp with baxandal bass/treble tone controls and volume and balance controls; 12A?7 gain recovery.

Victorian school PA System circa mid 1960's - Made by Australian Sound & T.V.. Combination radio, record-player, microphone input, monitor speaker, PA amplifier in free-standing steel cabinet. Features high quality construction, Woden MT101 mic transformer and 12AX7 preamp, EF86-12AU7-quad 6CA7 PP amplifier with A&E 2096 PT, and 2596 OT, and 6AL3 plate-ground catch diodes for PP stage. Essential school additions include a Bell pot and EF86 relaxation oscillator bell generator module with rate and decay pots, plus Garrard TA MkII for playing the national anthem.

Australian Sound Systems A13 amplifier circa 1946-7. Australian Sound Systems from Melbourne closed in 1947. Three input (MIC, PU, Radio) channel PA amplifier. 6J7 anode load RC tone. Voltage divider PI common cathode biased 6SN7. 6V6G cathode biased PP.
